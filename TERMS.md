# Purpose

This document defines the canonical terminology used throughout the **Engineering Trustworthy Industrial AI** research initiative.

Its objectives are to:

- Establish a consistent vocabulary.
- Prevent ambiguity across articles, talks, frameworks, and software.
- Provide authoritative definitions for concepts introduced by this research.
- Encourage consistent citation and adoption.

Unless otherwise noted, definitions in this document supersede earlier drafts or informal usage.

---

# Canonical Terms

## Engineering Trustworthy Industrial AI

**Status:** Foundational Vision

**Definition**

An engineering discipline focused on designing Industrial AI systems that earn trust through engineering knowledge, grounded reasoning, governed decisions, human accountability, and operational rigor.

Trust is considered an engineered property rather than an assumed characteristic.

---

## The Governed Decision Layer

**Status:** Canonical Framework

**First Introduced**

Volume I — The Governed Decision Layer

**Definition**

The architectural layer between reasoning and operational action responsible for validating, governing, explaining, authorizing, and recording industrial decisions.

Its purpose is to ensure that recommendations become accountable operational decisions.

---

## ASSET-grade

**Status:** Canonical Framework

**Definition**

A quality standard describing industrial AI outputs that are:

- **A**uditable
- **S**pecific
- **S**tandards-aligned
- **E**xplainable
- **T**rusted

ASSET-grade is intended as a practical benchmark for determining whether AI-generated recommendations are suitable for operational decision support.

The acronym is fixed.

No additional letters should be appended.

---

## Earned Autonomy

**Status:** Canonical Concept

**Definition**

The principle that autonomous capability should be granted progressively through demonstrated evidence of trustworthy performance, rather than configured upfront.

Autonomy is earned through validation, governance, operational experience, and human confidence.

---

## Grounded Reasoning

**Status:** Foundational Concept

**Definition**

Reasoning that is constrained and informed by engineering knowledge, physical principles, operational context, standards, and validated domain expertise rather than statistical correlation alone.

Grounded reasoning precedes trustworthy decisions.

---

## Engineering Knowledge

**Status:** Foundational Concept

**Definition**

The accumulated body of engineering expertise—including standards, procedures, asset knowledge, failure mechanisms, operational practices, physical models, and institutional experience—that provides the foundation for trustworthy Industrial AI.

---

## Trustworthy Decision

**Status:** Foundational Concept

**Definition**

A decision that is sufficiently explainable, grounded, governed, auditable, and operationally appropriate for industrial use.

Trustworthiness is an emergent property created by multiple engineering disciplines rather than any single AI model.

---

## Deterministic Decision Envelope

**Status:** Working Term

**Definition**

A deterministic operational boundary surrounding non-deterministic AI reasoning, ensuring that operational commitments remain predictable, accountable, and governed.

This term refers to deterministic operational decisions—not deterministic AI models.

---

## "Who Signs?"

**Status:** Signature Question

**Definition**

A recurring design test used to evaluate whether responsibility for an AI-assisted decision has been clearly identified.

If no accountable person, process, or authority can sign off on a decision, the system has not yet achieved operational readiness.
